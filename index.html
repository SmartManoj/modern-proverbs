Modern Proverbs

Old proverbs, corrected — the wisdom kept, the wrong word fixed.

Fast and steady wins the race.

Slow and steady wins the race.

Steadiness is the real virtue, not slowness. Between two equally consistent runners, the faster one wins — "slow" was never the point.

Succinctness is the soul of wit.

Brevity is the soul of wit.

Brevity is just short; succinctness is short and complete. You can be brief and lose the point — wit needs the point to survive.
